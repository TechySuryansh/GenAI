# Customer Service Best Practices for Retention Calls

## Call Opening
- Greet the customer warmly and introduce yourself by name
- Reference the customer's history: "I see you've been with us for [X] months, and we truly value your loyalty"
- State the purpose clearly: "I'm reaching out because we want to make sure you're getting the best value from your plan"

## Active Listening
- Let the customer express any frustrations without interruption
- Acknowledge their concerns: "I completely understand your concern about [issue]"
- Take notes on specific pain points to address
- Never argue or get defensive — empathize first, solve second

## Presenting Retention Offers
- Frame offers as exclusive: "I have a special offer available only for valued customers like yourself"
- Present the strongest offer first — don't start with a weak offer and negotiate up
- Explain the total value: "This package would normally cost $X, but I can offer it to you for $Y"
- Create urgency: "This offer is available for the next 48 hours"

## Handling Objections

### "Your prices are too high"
- Acknowledge the concern and ask what price range they're looking for
- Compare total value against competitors (speed, reliability, bundled services)
- Offer plan optimization or temporary discount
- Reference: "Many customers who switched to competitors came back because of our superior network"

### "I'm switching to a competitor"
- Ask which competitor and what offer they received
- Match or beat the competitor offer if within guidelines
- Highlight switching costs and service interruption risks
- Mention customer-specific benefits they would lose

### "I don't use all the services I'm paying for"
- Conduct a service audit and remove unnecessary add-ons
- Suggest a right-sized plan that better fits their usage
- Offer credit for unused services in the past billing cycle

## Handling Objections — Advanced

### "I've had too many service issues"
- Apologize sincerely and acknowledge the frustration
- Review the service history and explain what steps have been taken
- Offer a service credit for the inconvenience
- Schedule a proactive service check or technician visit
- Provide direct contact for future issues

### "I just don't need the service anymore"
- Explore the reason (moving, financial hardship, lifestyle change)
- Offer suspension of service instead of cancellation
- Provide a reduced-rate "keep alive" plan
- For financial hardship, offer temporary hardship discount program

## Call Closing
- Summarize the agreed-upon actions
- Confirm the customer's satisfaction with the resolution
- Provide your direct contact information for follow-up
- Thank them personally for their continued business
- Set a follow-up reminder for 30 days after intervention

## Ethical Guidelines
- Never make promises you cannot keep
- All discounts and offers must be within approved ranges
- Respect the customer's decision if they ultimately choose to leave
- Document all interactions and offers in the CRM system
- Ensure compliance with data privacy regulations at all times
- Be transparent about contract terms and any changes
